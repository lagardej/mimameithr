Naming authority for the Mimameithr project.

== Engine — <<norse-urtharbrunnr,Urtharbrunnr>>

[glossary]

[[engine-cadence]]Cadence::
Project term for <<domain-coupling-frequency,coupling frequency>>.
How often a <<domain-component,component>>'s <<domain-dynacore,DynaCore>> advances state.
Cadence is intrinsic to the component's physical domain — atmospheric circulation ticks faster than tectonic deformation.
Components with different cadences naturally stagger: a slower component consumes the latest state from a faster one on its next tick, without explicit coordination.

[[engine-domain]]Domain::
A bounded simulated entity with a set of <<domain-component,components>> bound to it.
Shape and topology are implementation details — a spherical planet and a flat plane world are both domains.
Components absent from the domain's configuration are not instantiated for it.
+
_Informal name:_ <<informal-heimr,Heimr>>.

[[engine-cellular-automaton]]Cellular Automaton::
A discrete model in which a grid of cells, each in one of a finite set of states, evolves in steps according to a fixed rule applied uniformly: each cell's next state depends only on its current state and the states of its neighbours.

== Model — <<norse-nornir,Nornir>>

[glossary]
[[domain-component]]Component::
A self-contained facade for a specific domain model.
Takes a <<domain-parameter,parameter>> set at initialization.
Hosts a <<domain-dynacore,dynamical core>> that advances <<domain-state,state>> over time.
Exposes that state as read-only.
Responds to <<domain-forcing,forcings>>.

[[domain-coupling-frequency]]Coupling Frequency::
Earth-system modelling term for how often a model <<domain-component,component>> exchanges state with its neighbours.
Used in <<domain-dynacore,dynamical core>> as the scientific basis for <<engine-cadence,cadence>>.

[[domain-dynacore]]Dynamical Core (DynaCore)::
The computational core of a <<domain-component,component>>.
Owns state, advances it over time, and receives <<domain-forcing,forcings>> from its component.
Callers interact with the component, not the dynamical core directly.

[[domain-forcing]]Forcing::
An external perturbation introduced into a running <<domain-dynacore,dynamical core>>.
The standard Earth-system term for a stressor applied from outside a <<domain-component,component>>'s own dynamics — solar output variations, volcanic aerosols, and designer-injected events are all forcings.

[[domain-parameter]]Parameter::
A <<domain-dynacore,dynamical core>> input.
Typed, validated value provided to a <<domain-component,component>> at initialization time.
Parameter is frozen once the dynamical core starts; runtime changes enter through <<domain-forcing,forcings>>.

[[domain-state]]State::
A <<domain-dynacore,dynamical core>> output.
An observable value exposed by a <<domain-component,component>> as it advances through time.
State is read-only to consumers; only the owning component may update it.

== Informal Names

[glossary]
[[informal-heimr]]Heimr::
Informal name for a <<engine-domain,Domain>>.
From Old Norse _heimr_ (world, home); one of the nine worlds of Yggdrasil.
Used in conversation and UI; the canonical engine term is Domain.


== Norse Naming

NOTE: Romanization convention: ð → th (e.g. _Urðr_ → Urth, _Verðandi_ → Verthandi).

[glossary]
[[norse-bragi]]Bragi — _Bragi_::
The front-end layer.
Named for Bragi Boddason, the archetypal early skald and figure of poetry; the storyteller; the visible, narrative-facing layer.

[[norse-edda]]Edda — _Edda_::
One universe iteration.
The written record of one world's story.
Broad, framing, and cosmological — the container for a world's mythic arc.

[[norse-kvasir]]Kvasir — _Kvasir_::
The science library.
Born from the mingled wisdom of all gods; the embodiment of knowledge.
Holds pure scientific and mathematical knowledge — geometry, physics, and other domain-agnostic science — shared across the simulation.

[[norse-mimameithr]]Mimameithr — _Mímameiðr_::
The umbrella project name.
Another name for Yggdrasil; the world-tree that survives Ragnarök; the structure that persists across universe deaths.

[[norse-nornir]]Nornir — _Nornir_::
Domain models / components.
The three weavers of fate who draw from the well; active shapers of simulation state.
See also: <<norse-urth,Urth>>, <<norse-verthandi,Verthandi>>, <<norse-skuld,Skuld>>.

[[norse-saga]]Saga — _Saga_::
A long-form story within a universe.
Narrative, historical, and continuous; follows the unfolding of lives, conflicts, and consequences across time.
Narrower than an <<norse-edda,Edda>>; more like the account of a people, lineage, place, or long-running sequence of events.

[[norse-seidr]]Seidr — _Seiðr_::
The computation layer.
The hidden practice by which reality is altered; opaque, rule-bound transformations applied to the world-state.
In the engine, Seidr executes the DynaCore kernels — pure mathematical procedures that reshape fields over time.
Not concerned with intent (<<norse-nornir,Nornir>>) nor structure (<<norse-urtharbrunnr,Urtharbrunnr>>), but with the act itself: the weaving of state through deterministic yet non-trivial operations.

[[norse-skuld]]Skuld — _Skuld_::
The Norn of the obligated future; what ought to happen.
Not what might happen — what is owed.
She holds the uncut thread. _Skuld_ shares its root with the English _should_.

[[norse-thattr]]Thattr — _Þáttr_::
A single strand within a <<norse-saga,Saga>>.
Shorter, narrower, and subordinate; a smaller episode or sub-story that sits within a larger whole.
Suits a single character arc, incident, or self-contained chapter inside a broader Saga or Edda.
Literally "strand" in Old Norse.

[[norse-urth]]Urth — _Urðr_::
The Norn of the past; what happened.
She does not draw from the well — she _is_ what has already been drawn.
What is fixed, what cannot be undone.

[[norse-urtharbrunnr]]Urtharbrunnr — _Urðarbrunnr_::
The engine / framework.
The Well of Urð at Mimameithr's roots; the source that feeds everything above it.

[[norse-verthandi]]Verthandi — _Verðandi_::
The Norn of the present; what is happening.
She does not record, she _acts_ — the thread moving through her fingers right now.

[[norse-volundr]]Volundr — _Vǫlundr_::
The developer toolbox.
The master smith of Norse legend; the maker of things, the one who forges the tools that others use.

== Topology

[glossary]
[[topology-cell]]Cell::
An opaque `CellId` identifier for one node in the grid hierarchy.
A cell has a resolution, a centre coordinate, a boundary polygon, a parent at any coarser resolution, and children at any finer resolution.
Validity is checked with `IGrid.IsValid`.

[[topology-grid]]Grid::
The spherical partition abstraction.
Provides deterministic, side-effect-free operations over a hierarchy of cells covering the sphere.
All coordinates are latitude/longitude in degrees; cell identifiers are opaque `CellId` values.
+
_Source type:_ `IGrid`.

[[topology-latlng]]LatLng::
A latitude/longitude coordinate in degrees, used as the common spatial currency of the topology layer.
+
_Source type:_ `LatLng`.

[[topology-resolution]]Resolution::
A discrete level in the cell hierarchy. `R0` is the coarsest level; higher values are finer.
The valid range and subdivision factor are backend-specific.
Components operate over a resolution range appropriate to their physical scale — macro processes use coarser resolutions; local detail uses finer ones.
+
_Source type:_ `Resolution`.
