= Engine architecture

== Deferred: scheduling optimization

Self-slotting can produce redundant or near-redundant jobs — e.g. a focus-triggered job for a region that the cadenced job is about to cover anyway, or two focus changes in quick succession each scheduling overlapping work. Deduplication, merging, or cancellation of redundant jobs is an **optimization problem, not a structural one**, and is explicitly deferred:

- Build the scheduler without any dedup/merge logic first. Redundant ticks are correctness-preserving (worst case: wasted work, not wrong results).
- Only design dedup/merge mechanisms once profiling shows redundancy is a measurable cost.

== Deferred: Fast-forward: folding N ticks into fewer

When the clock advances by a large Δ, a cadenced job may be due many times over (N pops) before `now`. Whether those N ticks can fold into fewer (Y < N) is a per-component decision:

- **Re-derivable components** (state is a pure function of slow inputs + current time, no memory): on being popped, compute state directly for the current clock time rather than for the stale `next-due`, then reschedule from there. N pops collapse to 1.
- **History-dependent components** (state depends on the path taken, not just current inputs): cannot fold: must step through intermediate ticks at their own cadence regardless of how large Δ is.

The fold decision and mechanism live in the component/DynaCore, not the scheduler — only the component knows which category it falls into. The scheduler simply advances the clock and hands the current time to whichever job is due; folding (if any) happens inside that job's tick.
