== Engine Constants

These are not negotiable.
Do not bring them up in design meetings as if they are.

=== Hex Grid

Hexagonal grid only.
Cleaner neighbour relationships than squares, no diagonal bullshit.
All spatial operations are defined over «topology-cell,Cells» at a given «topology-resolution,Resolution».

=== Hierarchical DGGS — 16 Resolutions

* `R0` covers the entire domain surface.
* Each `Rn` cell covers approximately 7 `R(n+1)` cells (H3 aperture-7 hierarchy).
* 16 resolution levels: `R0`–`R15`.
* Backend: Uber H3. The engine is unaware of this. `IGrid` is the only surface that matters.
If H3 ever gets replaced, Nornir and Brunnr must not notice.

=== Configuration Injection

All engine «domain-parameter,Parameters» are injected through configuration.
Supports file-based config and runtime UI-based config interchangeably.
No recompilation to tweak a simulation.
No hardcoded constants in domain code.
This is how you change the gravitational constant for a flat world without touching the engine.

== Scenarios

Scenarios are ordered from "boring and expected" to "what is wrong with you".
Each scenario must be expressible as a «engine-domain,Domain» configuration.
The engine must not require code changes to support any scenario listed here.

'''

=== 1. Standard Star System — Solar Analogue

*Purpose:* Benchmark.
If this doesn't work, nothing else matters.

The reference configuration.
One star, planets in stable orbits, at least one with conditions permissive to life.
Climate, hydrology, tectonics, and civilization emergence all exercised here.

*Configuration surface:*

* Star luminosity, spectral class
* Planetary orbital parameters (semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination)
* Planetary physical parameters (radius, mass, axial tilt, rotation period)
* Atmospheric composition
* Hydrosphere fraction

*Engine requirements:*

* Spherical «engine-domain,Domain» topology
* Standard insolation model (star at finite distance, orbiting body)
* All cadences from tectonic (slow) to atmospheric (fast) '''

=== 1c. Solar Analogue — Earth Equivalent, -5000 to +5000

*Purpose:* Civilization component full-window stress test.
Hydrosphere rapid-initialization stress test on a dry secondary domain.

Earth-equivalent planet as the primary domain.
Civilization window anchored at year 0 (present Earth equivalent), running from -5000 to +5000. Late neolithic at start; space age and interplanetary presence at end.
The civilization arc is flavour to anchor the scenario — the engine does not model a tech tree.

Mars analogue added as a secondary domain.
Begins completely dry.
Terraforming is initiated as a civilization-driven «domain-forcing,Forcing» late in the run.
The hydrosphere component on the Mars analogue must bootstrap from zero to a functional water cycle within ~100 years — orders of magnitude faster than geological hydrosphere formation.
This is the actual test.

*Configuration surface:*

* Primary domain: Earth-equivalent parameters (baseline from scenario 1)
* Simulation window: 10 000 years, offset to -5000 / +5000
* Secondary domain: Mars-analogue (lower mass, thin CO₂ atmosphere, zero initial hydrosphere)
* Terraforming forcing: onset year configurable; hydrosphere volume injected over a ~100-year ramp
* Inter-domain coupling: civilization state on primary drives terraforming forcing on secondary

*Engine requirements:*

* Simulation window offset is a configuration value — year zero is not special
* Hydrosphere component initializes correctly from zero (no water at t=0 is a valid state)
* Hydrosphere component stable under rapid forced initialization (~100-year timescale)
* Civilization state on one domain expressible as a «domain-forcing,Forcing» on another domain
* Lightweight multi-domain support (two domains, minimal coupling — this is not Nine Realms)

*Emergent behaviour of interest:*

* Hydrosphere stabilization curve on the Mars analogue — equilibrium, overshoot, or oscillation?
* Climate feedback loop on the Mars analogue once liquid water exists
* Civilisation-driven environmental pressure on the primary domain's own hydrosphere over 10 000 years '''

=== 1b. Nine Realms — As a Solar System

*Purpose:* Boundary check on the standard star system.
Same physics, knobs at 11.

The Nine Realms recast as a physically plausible (loosely) star system.
Each realm maps to an astronomical archetype with extreme but valid parameters.
One star.
Orbital mechanics apply.
The engine does not get to have opinions about what "reasonable" means.

*Configuration surface:*

* Per-realm orbital parameters pushed to physical extremes
* Binary/Trojan orbital configuration for Asgard &amp; Vanaheim
* Moon-parent relationship for Alfheim (tidal forcing source is a configured body, not the star)
* Subterranean domain flag for Svartalfheim (surface insolation is irrelevant; tidal flux is primary)

*Engine requirements:*

* Insolation model supports moon/exomoon geometry (parent body + star, not star only)
* Tidal heating as a configurable forcing source, independent of stellar distance
* Binary/co-orbital body support in the orbital mechanics model
* Extreme parameter values must not be clamped or silently corrected by the engine
* "Subterranean" domain mode: surface grid exists but stellar insolation contribution is zero '''

=== 2. Nine Realms — As Norse Mythology

*Purpose:* Multi-domain topology with causality-as-forcing.
Genuinely deranged.

// Promoted to its own tier of madness. Earned it.

Nine distinct realms («informal-heimr,Heimr») connected by Yggdrasil.
Not nine planets — nine domains with non-standard inter-domain relationships.
Asgard does not orbit anything.
Niflheim is cold because it is Niflheim, not because of orbital mechanics.

This scenario verifies that:

* The «engine-domain,Domain» model does not assume a single root domain
* Inter-domain «domain-forcing,Forcings» can represent mythological causality without a physical basis
* Climate components accept "it's just cold here" as a valid parameter set
* The engine does not panic when handed a topology that cannot be embedded in 3D space

*Configuration surface:*

* Per-realm physical constants (gravity, insolation, temperature baseline) as direct overrides
* Inter-realm coupling topology: Yggdrasil modelled as a forcing graph, not a physics graph
* Realm-specific atmospheric and hydrological overrides
* Forcing channels between realms (e.g. Niflheim cold flux into adjacent realms via Yggdrasil)

*Engine requirements:*

* Multi-domain support with explicit, configurable coupling topology
* Insolation model accepts an external constant flux (realm has no star; receives a configured value)
* All physically derived quantities must be overridable by configuration
* Forcing graph topology is not required to be planar, hierarchical, or physically motivated
* Domain count is a configuration value, not a compile-time constant

*Note:* Bifröst is a transport mechanism, not a climate forcing.

=== 3. Discworld

*Purpose:* Topology independence from geometry.

// GNU Terry Pratchett.
// We never read enough Pratchett. This scenario exists in his honour and is not up for debate.

A flat, circular disc.
Topology is still a valid «engine-domain,Domain» — the grid covers a surface, cells have neighbours, components advance state.
The geometry is just not a sphere.

The sun orbits the Discworld.
Not the other way around.
The sun is small and close.
This produces a climate model that is, charitably, unusual.

Obligatory note: the turtle and four elephants are load-bearing infrastructure and are explicitly out of scope.
Do not file a ticket.

*Configuration surface:*

* Domain geometry: flat disc (radius; thickness negligible)
* Solar body: orbital radius, orbital period, luminosity, orbital plane (equatorial — the sun circuits the rim)
* Gravity: constant vector field, downward, uniform across the surface
* Edge boundary condition: open — material exits at the Rim and is not reflected

*Configuration explicitly absent:*

* Axial tilt (meaningless on a disc)
* Coriolis effect (meaningless until "north" is defined; left as a future theological dispute)

*Engine requirements:*

* Non-spherical domain geometry. `IGrid` topology is unchanged; geometry is a rendering concern.
* Solar body component accepts an arbitrary orbital path, not only elliptical around the domain centre
* Climate component must not assume spherical geometry for insolation calculations
* Open boundary condition on domain edge

*Emergent behaviour of interest:* A permanent storm system anchored at the Hub (centre) is expected from first principles.
This is not configured.
If the engine produces it unprompted, that is a validation success and the round is on the engine.

'''

=== 4. High-Eccentricity Planet — Unpredictable Winters

*Purpose:* Extreme orbital forcing on climate and civilization components.

A planet with a highly eccentric orbit.
Winters are long, deep, and the precise timing is difficult to predict from within the simulation — civilizations do not have access to the orbital parameters.
"Winter is coming" is the design brief.

// "Or is it just a high eccentricity planet?" — yes. Yes it is. It's always just physics.
// The game designer spent three sentences independently deriving Kepler's second law.

*Configuration surface:*

* Orbital eccentricity (target: &gt;0.4; Earth is 0.017 for reference)
* Orbital period
* Perihelion/aphelion seasonal alignment
* Stellar luminosity (baseline temperature tuning)

*Engine requirements:*

* Insolation model correctly handles high eccentricity (large variation in star-planet distance over one orbit)
* Climate component responds to rapid insolation changes at perihelion passage
* Civilization component experiences resource pressure during extended aphelion winters
* No hardcoded year length anywhere — orbital period is a parameter

*Emergent behaviour of interest:*

* Civilization collapse/regression cycles synchronized with orbital period
* Agricultural stress during multi-year aphelion winters at extreme eccentricities
* In-simulation calendrical systems that fail to predict winter onset accurately '''

=== 5. Tidally Locked Planet

*Purpose:* Cadence scheduling stress.
Breaks any component that assumes a diurnal cycle.

One hemisphere in permanent day.
One in permanent night.
The terminator is a permanent weather front.
No sunrise, no sunset, no diurnal forcing signal — just a static thermal gradient from one side of the planet to the other.

*Configuration surface:*

* Rotation period equal to orbital period (synchronous rotation)
* Stellar flux (day-side only; night-side receives zero direct insolation)
* Atmospheric mass (determines how much heat the atmosphere redistributes to the night side)

*Engine requirements:*

* No component may assume a non-zero diurnal cycle
* Cadence scheduler must function when the primary periodic forcing (day/night) is absent
* Insolation model supports hemisphere-locked illumination
* Atmospheric heat redistribution between permanently lit and permanently dark hemispheres

*Emergent behaviour of interest:*

* Permanent storm system at the sub-stellar point (day side)
* Atmospheric collapse on the night side if atmospheric mass is too low (gases freeze out)
* Civilization constrained to the terminator band '''

=== 6. Archipelago World with Sea Level Rise

*Purpose:* Hydrology stress and civilization response to progressive forcing.

High ocean fraction, low continental fraction.
Land exists as archipelagos — no continents.
A configurable sea level rise forcing is applied during the simulation run, progressively drowning land area.
Waterworld is the failure state, not the starting configuration.

// Yes, this is Waterworld. No, Kevin Costner is not a simulation parameter.

*Configuration surface:*

* Continental fraction (target: low; ~10–15% land)
* Hydrosphere volume
* Sea level rise forcing: rate, onset time, total magnitude
* Archipelago generation parameters (island size distribution, elevation profile)

*Engine requirements:*

* Hydrological component handles near-zero continental fraction without degenerating
* Sea level rise expressible as a «domain-forcing,Forcing» applied to a running simulation
* Civilization component responds to progressive land loss (migration, contraction, collapse)
* Coastal boundary recalculation as sea level changes — topology does not change, cell habitability does

*Emergent behaviour of interest:*

* Civilization migration patterns as islands become uninhabitable
* Resource competition on shrinking land area
* Complete civilization collapse if land area drops below viability threshold before adaptation '''

=== 7. Dynamic Resolution Boundary Test

*Purpose:* Verify dynamic resolution degrades correctly at the minimum cell size floor.

Not a world scenario — a resolution stress test.
The engine enforces a minimum cell edge length of 100m.
This test verifies that the dynamic resolution system respects that floor, does not silently exceed it, and that components operating near the boundary behave correctly.

// This is an engineering test dressed as a scenario. It belongs here because if it fails,
// every other scenario that uses high-resolution detail is lying to you.

*Configuration surface:*

* Domain: standard spherical, Earth-scale
* Resolution policy: aggressive — push toward maximum resolution in a defined region
* Minimum cell edge length: 100m (engine floor; must be enforced, not assumed)

*Engine requirements:*

* Dynamic resolution system enforces the 100m minimum edge length floor
* No component receives a cell smaller than the floor, regardless of resolution policy
* Resolution transitions at the floor boundary are stable (no oscillation, no invalid cells)
* Floor violations are logged as errors, not silently clamped

*Pass criteria:*

* No cell below 100m edge length is ever instantiated
* Simulation remains stable when the resolution policy saturates at the floor
* Floor enforcement is observable in logs '''

== Exploratory Geometries

Not scenarios.
Geometry archetypes that the abstraction should not preclude.

* *Ring (Halo)* — cylindrical band.
Gravity is centrifugal.
Insolation is constant and lateral.
No poles.
* *Dyson Sphere* — full enclosure of a star.
Inner surface only.
Insolation uniform everywhere.
No day/night cycle by default.
* *Toroidal world* — surface wraps in both axes.
Topology is clean; gravity is not a solved problem.
Interesting edge case for neighbour relationships.
* *Inside-out (hollow sphere)* — civilization on the inner surface.
Central star.
No horizon.
Every geometry calculation inverts relative to a normal sphere. `IGrid` topology is identical; geometry is the only delta.
