= Mimameithr — Design Overview
:toc: left
:toclevels: 3

== Vision

Mimameithr is a civilisation-scale planetary simulation engine for imaginary worlds.
It models physical and civilisational processes over a horizon of tens of thousands of years — tectonics, climate, hydrology, geology, weather, vegetation, fauna, and the civilisations that emerge from and are constrained by them.

The simulation is not constrained to Earth-like worlds.
The player is free to define the physical characteristics of the world and its inhabitants.
A world of Pandora, Toril, or Arda is a valid target.

The engine is headless and testable without a UI.
Godot 4 is the rendering layer, kept strictly separate.

== Configuration model

Configuration is external to the engine and declarative.
Each entity exposes a set of *settings* — externally supplied parameters consumed by the underlying simulations.
Settings are distinct from *state*, which is computed by the engine from those settings and elapsed time.

Archetypes are pre-configured bundles of settings that the player can import and modify.
They exist to accelerate the configuration phase, not to constrain it.

== Phases

=== Generation phase

The generation phase is the setup stage.
It proceeds in two steps.

==== Planetary step

The player configures the star and the planetary system: number and type of bodies, orbits, rotation, and so on.

Bodies are not simulated from accretion.
The engine starts from a spherical body in a plausible post-accretion state.
The preliminary geological stabilisation — crust formation, meteoritic bombardment, ocean formation — is assumed to have already occurred off-engine.

Bodies in the same system may be at different geological maturities.
This is physically plausible: formation timing, body size, orbital position, tidal forcing, and late impacts all produce bodies at very different stages of evolution within a single system.
Geological maturity is therefore a per-body configuration parameter, not a global system constraint.

==== Component step

For a given body, the player adds and configures components: tectonics, elevation, geology, sea level, civilisation, and so on.

Components do not all operate on the same timescale and depend on one another.
Modifying one component triggers recomputation of dependent components.
During this step, modifications are applied immediately.

Time during the generation phase is local to each body.
The player can configure a body freely without affecting the simulation state of other bodies in the system.
This is analogous to the city management model in SimCity 4.

To accelerate multi-pass processes (tectonics, elevation, orogeny, climate, erosion, …), the engine supports temporal compression with a configurable range.
Two compression regimes are expected:

- A *geological regime* for system-level processes that require large timescales (millions of years per pass).
- An *observable regime* for processes the player watches in real time (days, seasons, centuries).

The appropriate range for each regime will be determined once the majority of systems are implemented.

=== Active phase

The active phase begins when the player declares the planetary system ready.
This declaration is the simulation epoch — the reference point from which all subsequent time is measured.

From this point, all bodies in the system advance together on a shared timeline.
The temporal compression range is reduced to correspond to the timescales of sentient history — tens of thousands of years at most.

The player may embody any body in the system and interact with it directly.

NOTE: The active phase, including the nature and characteristics of sentient inhabitants, is currently conceptual.
The generation phase is the immediate focus, as it defines the physical constraints within which any civilisation will emerge.
