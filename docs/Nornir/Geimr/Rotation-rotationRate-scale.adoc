= Rotation Rate Scale Reference

The `RotationRate` parameter uses a 1–100 scale that maps piecewise-exponentially from 10⁹ s to 10 s per full rotation, across three zones.
Unlike the other scales, the exponent *decreases* as score increases — a higher score means a shorter rotation period (faster spin):

[cols="<,<,<"]
|===
|Score range |Exponent range |Period range

|1 – 40 |10⁹ – 10⁵ |31.7 yr – 1.16 days
|40 – 70 |10⁵ – 10³ |1.16 days – 16.7 min
|70 – 100 |10³ – 10¹ |16.7 min – 10 s
|===

Score is linearly interpolated against exponent within each zone, then period = 10^exponent seconds.
Implemented by `Range100.PiecewiseExponentialScale(rotationRate, exponents: [9, 5, 3, 1])` (default thresholds `[40, 70, 100]`).

Bodies rotating faster than every 10 s (e.g. millisecond pulsars) exceed the scale maximum and are out of range for this simulation.
