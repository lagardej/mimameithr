= Concerns
:toc: left

NOTE: Elements are named after Old Norse terms. See `glossary.adoc` for the full list.

NOTE: Element boundaries are organizational, not permanent. Concerns may be split, merged, or reassigned as understanding evolves. Nothing here is a fixed contract — treat it as a current snapshot of intent, not a specification.

== Geimr

Celestial mechanics at body scale. Covers the astronomical properties of space bodies — their geometry, orbital dynamics, and rotation. Concerns here operate on body entities, not cells, and form a root dependency layer for other elements.

=== Body geometry

Physical dimensions and orientation of the planetary body. Feeds geometry-dependent computations such as irradiance distribution. Near-static; set at world generation.

=== Orbit

Position of the body relative to its star over time. Determines distance and orbital phase inputs to irradiance.

=== Body rotation

Rotational state of the body. Determines local time-of-day and drives day/night cycling.

== Eldr

Surface energy input at cell scale. Covers how stellar energy arrives at and is absorbed by each surface cell. Forms the thermal foundation for Lopt and Logr.

=== Stellar luminosity

Energy output of the star. An external driver with no dependencies inside the simulation; feeds all solar energy calculations downstream.

=== Irradiance

Solar energy received per surface cell, accounting for geometry, orbit, and rotation. The primary energy driver for all surface processes.

=== Albedo

Fraction of incoming solar energy reflected by each surface cell. Determined by surface composition and cover; feeds back into the energy balance.

== Hlothyn

Solid earth at cell scale. Covers tectonic processes, terrain formation, geological composition, and geothermal activity. The physical substrate on which all other cell-scale elements operate.

=== Tectonics

Crustal structure and plate dynamics. The foundational driver for most of Hlothyn — terrain, geology, geothermal heat, and seismic/volcanic activity all trace back here.

=== Orogeny

Mountain-building processes. Captures the uplift and decay of mountain belts as an intermediate between tectonic forces and final terrain shape.

=== Elevation

Surface height per cell. Synthesizes tectonic, orogenic, and isostatic inputs into the terrain geometry consumed by almost every other element.

=== Dynamics

Seismic and volcanic activity indices per cell. Captures hazard potential derived from tectonic boundary conditions; does not simulate individual events.

=== Sedimentation

Deposition and erosion of surface material over time. Connects terrain, water flow, and geological surface composition at shorter timescales than tectonics.

=== Geology

Surface rock character per cell. A snapshot of dominant geological type and age driven by tectonic history and orogenic processes.

=== Geothermal

Continuous background heat flux from the crust. Driven by volcanic activity and crustal properties; distinct from discrete volcanic events.

=== Surface temperature

Near-surface thermal state per cell at the broadest scale. Combines solar energy input and albedo into a baseline temperature used by atmospheric and other processes.

== Logr

Water at cell and body scale. Covers ocean basins, sea level, ocean circulation, river drainage, and groundwater. Driven by Eldr energy, Lopt precipitation, and Hlothyn terrain.

=== Sea Level

Global water level baseline. Influenced by ice volume; determines the land/ocean boundary for other elements.

=== Basin

Ocean basin geometry and distribution. Shapes circulation patterns and interacts with sea level and terrain.

=== Ocean Circulation

Large-scale movement of ocean water. Driven by thermal gradients, basin geometry, and sea level; redistributes heat and moisture across the sphere.

=== River Network

Freshwater drainage across the surface. Shaped by terrain and precipitation; connects surface water to groundwater.

=== Groundwater

Subsurface water storage and flow. Fed by precipitation and river infiltration; a slow-moving reservoir with broad ecological influence.

== Hrim

Ice and permafrost at cell scale. Covers ice sheets, glaciers, sea ice, and frozen ground. Modifies albedo, sea level, and river flux; feeds back into Lopt and Logr.

=== Ice Sheet

Large continental ice masses. Affect sea level, surface albedo, and terrain under ice load.

=== Glacier

Valley and mountain ice bodies. Shaped by terrain and thermal conditions; influence local elevation and river systems.

=== Sea Ice

Frozen ocean surface. Responds to thermal state; strongly affects surface albedo and ocean-atmosphere exchange.

=== Permafrost

Permanently frozen ground. Affects soil composition, drainage, and surface water behaviour in cold regions.

== Lopt

Atmospheric dynamics at cell scale. Covers air temperature, pressure, wind circulation, and weather patterns. Bridges Eldr energy input and Logr water movement.

=== Thermal Redistribution

Heat transport through the atmosphere. Takes surface energy input and distributes it across the sphere, driving circulation and precipitation.

=== Circulation

Large-scale atmospheric wind patterns. Shaped by thermal gradients, terrain, and atmospheric composition; drives weather and moisture transport.

=== Composition

Atmospheric gas make-up. Affects heat retention, circulation behaviour, and habitability.

=== Precipitation

Water delivery to the surface. Driven by circulation and thermal state; the primary freshwater input to Logr, Hrim, and Ithunn.

== Ithunn

Living systems at cell scale. Covers vegetation, fauna, soil biology, and disease. Constrained by Hlothyn, Logr, Lopt, and Hrim conditions.

=== Biome

Broad ecological zone classification per cell. Synthesizes climate and terrain inputs into the dominant life-supporting context from which more detailed biological concerns derive.

=== Flora

Plant life distribution and density. Shaped by biome, soil, and water availability; feeds back into soil character and surface cover.

=== Soil

Biologically active surface layer. Built by flora and sedimentation; mediates water retention and nutrient cycling.

=== Fauna

Animal life distribution and density. Follows flora and biome conditions; interacts with disease dynamics.

=== Disease

Pathogen and disease vector distribution. Driven by fauna density, water access, and atmospheric circulation.

== Mann

Sentient civilization at cell and network scale. Covers settlement, resource extraction, infrastructure, and cultural dynamics. Emerges from and is constrained by all other elements.

== Projections

Read-only aggregations derived from one or more elements. No simulation logic — pure views over existing concern state. Do not belong to any element.

=== Surface cover

Composite per-cell surface type derived from whichever elements expose a surface state. Consumed primarily by Albedo.
